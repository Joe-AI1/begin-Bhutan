Paro Taktsang

Paro Taktsang is a wonderful cliffside monastery, known as the Tiger's Nest, offering stunning views of the Paro Valley.

Drukgyel Dzong

Drukgyel Dzong is an ancient fortress and a UNESCO World Heritage site. The site holds historical significance in Bhutan’s history.

Thimphu

The capital city of Bhutan, Thimphu offers cultural landmarks such as the Tashichho Dzong and the National Memorial Chorten.

Bumthang Valley

This mystical valley is home to historical temples, religious sites, and trekking paths, offering a deep sense of Bhutanese history.

Kila Nunnery

Kila Nunnery, perched on a cliff, is a peaceful retreat for Buddhist nuns, with beautiful views of the Paro Valley.

Paro Tsechu

One of Bhutan's most famous festivals, Paro Tsechu features colorful mask dances and ancient Buddhist traditions.

Lhakhang Karpo and Lhakhang Nagpo

These historic temples are associated with the founding of Bhutan. They are beautifully preserved and offer spiritual solace.

Language in Bhutan

Bhutan has a linguistic diversity where language plays an important role in the culture and traditions of the country. The main and most important language is Dzongkha, which is the official language of the country. In addition, there are various local languages and dialects spoken in Bhutan, which include:

  1. Dzongkha: The official language used in education, administration, and national communication.
  2. Languages and Dialects: Sharchop, Lhotshamkha, and Tibetan are also spoken in different regions of Bhutan.
  3. Languages Influenced by Neighboring Regions: Other languages, including English, are used in education and business in Bhutan.
