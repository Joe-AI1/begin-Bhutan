Geography

Bhutan is a landlocked country located in South Asia, nestled between China to the north and India to the south. The country features diverse geographical highlights, including high mountains in the Himalayas, with an average elevation of about 3,000 meters (9,800 feet) above sea level. Bhutan is also home to several rivers, such as the Wang Chhu and Torsa Chhu, which flow through the country.

The climate in Bhutan varies with elevation: higher areas experience cold temperatures, while lower regions are warmer and more humid. Additionally, Bhutan boasts a rich biodiversity due to its dense forests and varied ecosystems, making it one of the ecologically significant countries in the region.

Bhutan’s climate varies significantly depending on altitude and can be divided into three main zones:

  1. Alpine Climate: In areas above 3,000 meters (9,800 feet), such as the northern Himalayas, the climate is cold year-round. Winters are marked by heavy snowfall, and temperatures can drop below freezing. Thimphu, the capital, experiences mild winters and cold but refreshing summers.
  2. Subtropical Climate: At lower altitudes, including places like Punakha (1,200 meters or 3,900 feet), the weather is warmer, more humid, and subtropical. Temperatures here are mild, and it is more comfortable year-round.
  3. Temperate Climate: Bhutan’s central areas, including Bumthang and Trongsa, experience temperate weather, with a distinct monsoon season.
