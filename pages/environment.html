Environment

Bhutan Wildlife

The national animal of Bhutan is the Takin. It has characteristics similar to both goats and cattle, with a distinctive large head and a strong body. The Takin is considered a symbol of the country and is well-conserved in Bhutan.

Bhutan's Natural Sustainability

The national tree of Bhutan is the Bhutanese Cypress, also known as "Hing Singye Wangchuck." This species is culturally and environmentally significant in the country. Bhutan has a strong connection with nature, and this tree symbolizes sustainability and the conservation of forests in the region.

Bhutanese Cuisine

The national dish of Bhutan is Ema Datshi, which is a dish made from chili peppers and cheese. It is known for its spiciness and rich flavor and is a popular dish in Bhutan. Ema Datshi symbolizes the culinary culture of the Bhutanese people and is often served with rice.
